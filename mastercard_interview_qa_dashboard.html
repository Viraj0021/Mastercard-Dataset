Q2
How do you make visualizations user-friendly?
Clear hierarchy, consistent color coding, minimal clutter, and logical grouping of related metrics. Tooltips let users explore details without overwhelming the main view.
Visual hierarchy Color coding Tooltips Clean layout Minimal labels
Q3
How do you present insights to non-technical stakeholders?
Lead with the headline and the "so what." For Mastercard: "Stock grew from $9 to $528 over 18 years, driven by a 45.7% profit margin." Avoid jargon, use plain labels, and let visuals carry the story. Choose charts based on what's easiest to read, not what's most technically impressive.

Q4
What would you improve in a poorly designed dashboard?
Too much information → Top 5–6 metrics only
Inconsistent colors → Assign meaning per color
Missing context → Add YoY comparisons
No clear flow → Summary → detail layout
